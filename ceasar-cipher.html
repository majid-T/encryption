Ceasar cipher method for Encryption

Ceasar cipher method is a method used by Julius Ceasar, the Roman general to cipher his messages and letters to his officers and allies. He will pick a key or use the the lenght of first word as a key, then shift each letter in the phrase in alphabetic order upwards to generate a new letter. For example letter a with key of 3 will become a + 3 resulting to d. Below is a small demonstration of how it works. Also the weakness of ths method was the letter distribution in latin language. A smart enemy general would now that some letters are more common in some languages. for example letters a, o and u or words such as the are more likely to be found in a phrase. By using this technic and counting the difference to original letter and trying out the options this code can be break easily.
